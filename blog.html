Title: To Eat or Not to Eat (at the University of Tampa)

Culinary Adventures at the University of Tampa: A Guide to Campus Eateries

 

            The University of Tampa is known for its warm weather, laidback atmosphere and highly ranked educational programs but today, we are going to talk about the culinary scene at this private institution. As students embark on their academic journeys, it's essential to explore not just the classrooms but also the array of dining options that contribute to the rich overall campus experience. In this blog post, we'll take a culinary tour of some notable restaurants on the University of Tampa campus, highlighting the favourites (and not so favourites) for the inside scoop on to eat or not to eat at the University of Tampa. Who’s hungry?

 

Dining Options

The University of Tampa boasts over 15 different options when it comes to where to eat on campus. I will break down each one for you, telling you what they serve and telling you what I think about them. Stay tuned ‘til the end for a reveal of the top voted eatery on campus!

 

Chick-fil-a

  • Chick-fil-A, a beloved fast-food chain, has found its home on the University of Tampa campus. Known for its crispy chicken sandwiches and waffle fries, this eatery is a favourite among students. The welcoming service and commitment to quality make it a popular choice for a quick and satisfying meal.

The Grill

  • At The Grill, patrons can enjoy top-notch cheeseburgers, chicken tenders, grilled chicken sandwiches, along with classic accompaniments like French fries and onion rings. Additionally, there's a selection of vegetarian choices and healthier alternatives to cater to various preferences.

 

Fresh Creations

  • Craving a specially crafted salad or a wholesome wrap? Then Fresh Creations is the way to go. Your taste buds may dance with delight as our skilled salad specialists whip up your choice of a freshly made salad or a yummy wrap. This is a popular choice for more health-conscious students who prefer a fresher bite.

La Havana

  • La Havana is a Cuban inspired eatery that has traditional Cuban food like mojo pork and fried plantain. This dining option has a familiar feeling for many of the staff and students since Tampa is home to many Cuban Americans who have helped to make Tampa what it is today.

Simply to go

  • Pressed for time? Explore Simply To Go! This convenient option provides a daily selection of freshly prepared salads and sandwiches for those on the go.

 

Starbucks                                                    

  • Starbucks Reserve creates a welcoming and laid-back environment, both indoors and on the patio, designed for the University of Tampa community. This establishment features Starbucks Reserve, a unique assortment of special, small-batch coffees carefully curated from farms worldwide. Alongside, indulge in an array of pastries and sweet and savoury treats, and explore a selection of coffee-related accessories and equipment. To add to the experience, Starbucks Reserve also sells a premium line of Teavana Teas.

 

Dairy queen/ orange Julius

  • The Dairy Queen/Orange Julius Treat Centre, a pioneering initiative on a college campus, is dedicated to offering a delightful array of your beloved Dairy Queen and Orange Julius treats including, ice cream, blizzards, hot dogs and smoothies.

 

Star ginger

  • Explore an array of fresh and vibrant twists on traditional Pan-Asian cuisine, featuring an array of dishes such as Pho Noodle Bowls, Vegan-Friendly Banh Mi Sandwiches, Stir-Fry Rice Bowls, Curries, Pot Stickers, and many other delectable options!

 

Pandini’s

  • Pandini’s elevates Old World Italian cuisine to new heights, offering individual gourmet and classic pizzas, along with signature parmesan subs. The experience is enhanced with fresh salads and Italian favourites, including the option to customize your own tossed pasta*.

 

Gourmet grocer

  • Gourmet Grocer is a small, boutique-style market providing an array of fresh gourmet sandwiches, pastries, salads, convenience items, premium bottled beverages, and an assortment of other delights.

 

Salsa Rico

  • Salsa Rico is a Mexican style grill, inspired by the laid-back lifestyle of the Californian coast. Here, you can indulge in clear and fresh flavours presented in a comfortable and unhurried atmosphere. This “chipotle-esque” eatery serves up burrito bowls, salads, tacos, quesadillas and burritos with traditional tortilla chips and all the “fixin’s”.

 

Mindful

  • Mindful is a great option for early risers or those who love a good breakfast for dinner (or lunch). They serve a variety of freshly made breakfast options, including scrambled egg bowls, avocado toast, coffee and bakery items.

 

Ultimate dining

  • Indulge in a diverse selection of fresh and appetizing dishes crafted to cater to every palate, rivalling the offerings of well-known restaurants. Whether it's breakfast, lunch, or dinner, ultimate dining is available with an expansive all-you-can-eat buffet, boasting 10 dining stations to satisfy your culinary cravings.

 

AFC sushi

  • AFC sushi, you guessed it, serves sushi. They offer artfully crafted sushi classics, including California rolls, eel rolls, teriyaki chicken rolls, assorted platters and even seaweed salad (all made in house). For those seeking vegetarian options, vegetable rolls are also on the menu.

 

The Rathskeller

  • Seeking a serene spot to enjoy a meal while hitting the books? Ditch the library and explore the Rathskeller, situated in the basement of Plant Hall. Sounds scary I know but it’s not that bad (we can’t promise it’s not haunted though, as many students believe it is). This dining hotspot is well-frequented and hosts a Boar’s Head Deli along with Starbucks drinks available.

 

Einstein Bros. Bagels

  • For those who believe that breakfast is the most important meal of the day, Einstein Bros. Bagels is a haven. From classic bagels with cream cheese to hearty breakfast sandwiches, this spot ensures students start their day on a delicious note. The inviting aroma of freshly baked bagels is an irresistible morning greeting.
  • Beyond its renowned bagels, cream cheese spreads, and coffee, Einstein Bros. Bagels provides an extensive selection of salads, sandwiches, and baked treats, ensuring it's an ideal destination at any hour of the day.

 

Panache

  • Panache stands as an elegant dining establishment, presenting a sophisticated fusion of contemporary cuisine. It takes diverse global flavours, infuses them with creativity, and imparts a truly memorable twist. The only time most students visit this eatery is if they are lucky enough to be invited by a faculty/ staff member for a lunch meeting.

 

*They replaced the pasta station with Aussie Outback grill officially as of October 23, 2023.

 

 

What the Health?! Availability for Dietary Restrictions

            Whether you are a vegan, vegetarian, have dietary restrictions based on religion, diseases, allergies, etc., the University of Tampa has options for you, or at least they try to. Pretty much every dining option on campus has vegetarian options (even if that option is just a side like fries or rice), but it is extremely limited. You can speak to dining services if you require special accommodations regarding allergies but many students (myself included) think that the university needs to have a wider range of options for people who do not stick to a mainstream unlimited diet.

            In addition to needing more options for alternative dietary lifestyles, most students agree that the university needs more options for students who prioritize health and nutrition over a quick bite. While UT does offer some healthier options and ways to build a nutritious meal, most dining options are fried carb heavy meals that are not conducive to everyday healthy eating.

 

 

What’s the Cost?

            Students who reside on campus have 3-4 options for meal plan but are required to have at lease one. They range in price from $1692 to $3136 per semester with a varying number of meals per week from different on campus eateries.

            For those who live off campus and still want to grab a bite to eat between classes or before heading home, there are also plans available for you. They range in price from $195 to $420 per semester also with a varying amount t of meals for the entire semester.

The university also offers “UT” dollars that are equivalent to their dollar amount ranging from $50 to $499 dollars. Unlike the meal plans and spartan dollars, UT dollars never expire and can be used at any eatery on campus.

The University of Tampa campus is not only a place of academic growth but also a melting pot of flavours. Whether you're in the mood for a quiet coffee, a hearty breakfast, a crispy chicken sandwich, or an exploration of global cuisines, the campus has something for everyone. While there is obviously still (a lot of) room for improvement, the university tries its best to cater to all its students, staff, faculty and guests in the best way. So next time you get hungry, look around at the options UT has provided. Bon appétit!
